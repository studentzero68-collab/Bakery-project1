Chocolate Chip Cookies

These cookies hit harder than a final boss. You've been warned.
15 min prep 12 min bake
What it means when you make it: Comfort and care. The classic way of saying "I was thinking about you."
Family night Friend hangout Cosy date
Ingredients
  • 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups chocolate chips
Steps
  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C) and line your baking sheets.
  2. Beat butter and both sugars together until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in eggs and vanilla.
  4. Gradually mix in flour, baking soda and salt.
  5. Stir in the chocolate chips.
  6. Drop rounded tablespoons onto baking sheets.
  7. Bake 9 to 11 minutes until golden. Let cool — patience is a skill.

Lava Cake

The molten centre is basically the plot twist of desserts — you never see it coming.
20 min prep 12 min bake
What it means when you make it: Passion that runs deep. Warm on the inside even when it looks composed on the outside.
Romantic dinner Impress a friend
Ingredients
  • 200g dark chocolate
  • 100g butter
  • 3 eggs and 3 yolks
  • 100g icing sugar
  • 60g flour
  • Pinch of salt
Steps
  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Butter and flour 4 ramekins.
  2. Melt chocolate and butter together. Let it cool slightly.
  3. Whisk eggs, yolks and sugar until pale.
  4. Fold chocolate mixture into eggs, then fold in flour.
  5. Fill ramekins three quarters full.
  6. Bake 10 to 12 minutes — edges set, centre still jiggles.
  7. Rest 1 minute then invert onto a plate. Serve immediately.

Fluffy Pancakes

Making these before 9am? You are the protagonist. Everyone else is NPC.
10 min prep 15 min cook
What it means when you make it: A soft layered effort. The kind that says "I woke up early because you matter."
Sunday family Breakfast in bed
Ingredients
  • 1½ cups flour
  • 3½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1¼ cups milk
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tbsp melted butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla
Steps
  1. Sift all your dry ingredients into a bowl.
  2. Make a well in the middle and pour in the milk, egg, butter and vanilla. Mix until just combined — lumps are fine.
  3. Let the batter rest for 5 minutes. This is the secret checkpoint.
  4. Cook on medium heat, a quarter cup per pancake. Flip when bubbles form on the surface.
  5. Stack high and serve with syrup, berries or whatever your side quest requires.

Banana Bread

Those overripe bananas were not a mistake. They were training for this moment.
15 min prep 60 min bake
What it means when you make it: Turning something past its prime into something beautiful. A second chance baked into a loaf.
Wholesome family Study session fuel
Ingredients
  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • ⅓ cup melted butter
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 1 egg beaten
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1½ cups flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • Pinch of salt
Steps
  1. Preheat oven to 175°C and grease a loaf tin.
  2. Mix the melted butter into the mashed banana.
  3. Stir in the sugar, egg and vanilla.
  4. Mix in the flour, baking soda and salt. Don't overmix.
  5. Pour into the tin and bake 55 to 65 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean.
  6. Cool before slicing. The wait builds character.

Cinnamon French Toast

Stale bread just got a glow up. This is what redemption looks like.
5 min prep 10 min cook
What it means when you make it: Turning the ordinary into something special with very little. A reminder that effort changes everything.
Weekend mornings Lazy morning date
Ingredients
  • 4 slices of bread
  • 2 eggs
  • ¼ cup milk
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Butter for the pan
Steps
  1. Whisk eggs, milk, cinnamon, vanilla and sugar together in a shallow bowl.
  2. Dip each slice of bread into the mixture and let it soak for a few seconds on each side.
  3. Melt butter in a pan over medium heat.
  4. Cook each slice 2 to 3 minutes per side until golden.
  5. Serve with syrup, fruit or powdered sugar.
