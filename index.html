Chocolate Chip Cookies

These cookies hit harder than a final boss. You've been warned.
15 min prep 12 min bake
What it means when you make it: Comfort and care. The classic way of saying "I was thinking about you."
Family night Friend hangout Cosy date
Ingredients
  • 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups chocolate chips
Steps
  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C) and line your baking sheets.
  2. Beat butter and both sugars together until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in eggs and vanilla.
  4. Gradually mix in flour, baking soda and salt.
  5. Stir in the chocolate chips.
  6. Drop rounded tablespoons onto baking sheets.
  7. Bake 9 to 11 minutes until golden. Let cool — patience is a skill.

Lava Cake

The molten centre is basically the plot twist of desserts — you never see it coming.
20 min prep 12 min bake
What it means when you make it: Passion that runs deep. Warm on the inside even when it looks composed on the outside.
Romantic dinner Impress a friend
Ingredients
  • 200g dark chocolate
  • 100g butter
  • 3 eggs and 3 yolks
  • 100g icing sugar
  • 60g flour
  • Pinch of salt
Steps
  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Butter and flour 4 ramekins.
  2. Melt chocolate and butter together. Let it cool slightly.
  3. Whisk eggs, yolks and sugar until pale.
  4. Fold chocolate mixture into eggs, then fold in flour.
  5. Fill ramekins three quarters full.
  6. Bake 10 to 12 minutes — edges set, centre still jiggles.
  7. Rest 1 minute then invert onto a plate. Serve immediately.
